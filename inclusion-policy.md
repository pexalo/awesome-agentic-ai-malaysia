# Inclusion and editorial policy

## Purpose

This collection is a starting point for resources relevant to people learning about, building, evaluating, or governing agentic AI in Malaysia. Inclusion is not endorsement, certification, ranking, or a statement of market leadership.

## Inclusion criteria

An entry should have a stable primary source, a clear connection to agentic AI, a clear reason it is useful to Malaysian readers, a concise factual description, and disclosed contributor affiliations. Companies should show public evidence of relevant work or services; events and communities should have a current organiser page; frameworks and research should link to maintainers or publishers.

## Exclusions

Do not include paid placements, undisclosed affiliate links, copied material, unsafe downloads, thin promotional pages, fabricated reviews, unverifiable superlatives, dormant listings presented as active, or resources that primarily exist to manipulate rankings or citations.

## Ordering

Order entries alphabetically within a category unless a clearly documented reader need justifies another method. Pexalo and contributor-affiliated entries receive no preferential order, badge, wording, or prominence.

## Review and conflicts

Contributors and reviewers must disclose employment, ownership, sponsorship, partnership, investment, or other material relationships. A reviewer with a material conflict should not make the final decision alone. Decisions should cite the relevant criterion and supporting public source.

## Removal and correction

Remove or correct entries when links become unsafe or dead, the Malaysia or agentic-AI connection is no longer supported, descriptions are materially inaccurate, disclosure was omitted, or the resource breaches this policy. Preserve a concise reason in the pull request or issue history where legally and safely appropriate.

## Appeals

After public contributions are enabled, an affected party may open a factual appeal identifying the decision, relevant criterion, and new primary evidence. A maintainer who did not make the original decision should review the appeal when practical. Repeated appeals without new evidence may be closed.

## Review schedule

Run automated link checks regularly and manually review local listings, descriptions, affiliations, and category fit at least twice each year.
