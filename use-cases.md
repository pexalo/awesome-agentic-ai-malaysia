# Awesome Agentic AI Malaysia: use cases

These are design patterns, not promises of results. A real implementation requires local discovery, controls, testing, and measurement.

## Framework discovery

**Situation:** A Malaysian development team needs to compare agent frameworks without relying on promotional summaries.

**Documented workflow:** Start with the framework category, follow primary documentation, and compare orchestration, tool use, memory, evaluation, and deployment requirements.

**Expected value:** A faster, more defensible shortlist.

**Controls to consider:** Verify version-specific claims in the linked primary documentation and run a small proof of concept.

## Executive orientation

**Situation:** A business leader needs a practical introduction to AI agents before commissioning a project.

**Documented workflow:** Read the overview, glossary, FAQ, governance resources, and local use cases in that order.

**Expected value:** Shared language for discussing objectives, limits, risk, and ownership.

**Controls to consider:** Treat examples as educational, not as a substitute for workflow analysis or professional advice.

## Research and education

**Situation:** A lecturer or student is assembling an agentic AI reading list.

**Documented workflow:** Use research and learning categories, prioritising papers and official documentation, then record publication date and version.

**Expected value:** A source-led syllabus rather than a list of secondary opinions.

**Controls to consider:** Check licensing before reproducing diagrams, datasets, or text.

## Community discovery

**Situation:** A practitioner wants to find Malaysian meetups, programmes, or peer groups.

**Documented workflow:** Review community listings, confirm the organiser and current event page, then contact organisers directly.

**Expected value:** More visible and connected local ecosystem activity.

**Controls to consider:** Remove dormant or misleading listings during periodic review.

## Provider research

**Situation:** An organisation is researching local providers.

**Documented workflow:** Use neutral provider entries as discovery points, then validate capabilities through public case studies, documentation, references, and a scoped evaluation.

**Expected value:** A more evidence-based procurement process.

**Controls to consider:** The repository does not endorse providers or guarantee outcomes.

## Use-case qualification checklist

1. Name the workflow owner and the current problem.
2. Measure current volume, time, cost, errors, and exceptions.
3. Identify systems, data, permissions, and policy constraints.
4. Choose the lowest-complexity design that can deliver the outcome.
5. Define approvals, fallbacks, incident ownership, and stop criteria.
6. Pilot on representative cases and compare with the baseline.
7. Expand only after quality, safety, reliability, and value are demonstrated.
